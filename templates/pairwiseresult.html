Pairwise Sequence Alignment

Pairwise Sequence Alignment is used to identify regions of similarity that may indicate functional, structural and/or evolutionary relationships between two biological sequences (protein or nucleic acid).

Why pairwise alignment ?

  • Used to compute evolutionary distances, which are used to build phylogenetic tree.
  • Used for sequence assembly in shotgun sequencing.
  • Underlines multiple alignment, which is used to find consensus patterns.
